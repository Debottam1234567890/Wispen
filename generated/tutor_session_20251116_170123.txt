That's a *fantastic* definition, Debottam! You've captured the core concepts perfectly.

Here's a slightly more formal definition, but it's essentially what you just said:

**Machine Learning (ML) is a field of computer science that allows computers to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.**

*   **Experience/Data:** The more data the machine has, the better it becomes at identifying patterns and making predictions.
*   **Patterns:** Machine learning algorithms identify complex patterns within the data.
*   **Application to Unknown Data:** Once trained, the model can then make predictions or decisions about new, unseen data.
