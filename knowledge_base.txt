NEWTON'S LAWS OF MOTION
-----------------------

LAW 1 - INERTIA
Direct Explanation:
"An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion, unless acted upon by a force."

What This Means:
- Your body jerks forward when a bus stops suddenly (inertia!)
- Objects in space keep moving forever (no friction)
- Heavier objects are harder to move or stop

Real Example:
"When you pull a tablecloth quickly, dishes stay in place due to inertia!"

LAW 2 - F = ma
Direct Explanation:
Force equals mass times acceleration.

What This Means:
- Heavier objects need more force to move
- Same force â†’ lighter object accelerates more
- This is why pushing a car is hard but pushing a bike is easy!

Formula: F = ma
- F = Force (Newtons)
- m = mass (kg)
- a = acceleration (m/sÂ²)

Example:
Push a 10kg box with 50N force:
a = F/m = 50/10 = 5 m/sÂ²

LAW 3 - ACTION-REACTION
Direct Explanation:
"For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

What This Means:
- When you jump, you push Earth down (action), Earth pushes you up (reaction)
- Rockets work by pushing gas down, gas pushes rocket up
- When you swim, you push water back, water pushes you forward

Fun Fact:
"When you sit on a chair, you push down with your weight, the chair pushes up with equal force - that's why you don't fall through!"

- Neutralized by acids

- 0-6: Acidic (lemon = 2)
- 7: Neutral (water)

TAJ MAHAL
---------

WHO BUILT IT:
Emperor Shah Jahan (Mughal ruler of India)

WHEN:
Built between 1632-1653 (took 22 years!)

WHY:
Shah Jahan built it as a memorial for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died giving birth to their 14th child in 1631. He was heartbroken and wanted to create the most beautiful monument ever.

FACTS:
- Located in Agra, India
- Made of white marble that changes color with light
- 20,000 workers and 1,000 elephants used
- Cost about 32 million rupees (equivalent to billions today)
- Name means "Crown of Palaces" in Persian
- One of the Seven Wonders of the World

INTERESTING STORY:
"Legend says Shah Jahan wanted to build a black Taj Mahal for himself across the river, but he was imprisoned by his son before he could. He spent his last years gazing at the Taj Mahal from his prison window."

   - Wanted democratic representation

- Abolished feudalism

1792: Republic Established
- Monarchy abolished

- Robespierre led radical phase

--------------------

Prince Hamlet of Denmark sees his father's ghost, who reveals he was murdered by Hamlet's uncle Claudius. Claudius has now married Hamlet's mother and become king. The ghost demands revenge.

   - Hamlet contemplates suicide
   - Everyone dies in the end

4. Corruption:
   - "Something is rotten in the state of Denmark"
   - Claudius's crime corrupts the whole kingdom

LITERARY DEVICES
----------------

METAPHOR:
Comparing two things without using "like" or "as"
Example: "Time is money" (time isn't literally money, but it's valuable)

SIMILE:
Comparing using "like" or "as"
Example: "She was as brave as a lion"

PERSONIFICATION:
Giving human qualities to non-human things
Example: "The wind whispered through the trees"

SYMBOLISM:
Object represents a bigger idea
Example: Dove = peace, Red rose = love

IRONY:
Opposite of what's expected
- Verbal irony: Saying opposite of what you mean (sarcasm)
- Situational irony: Outcome opposite of expectation
- Dramatic irony: Audience knows something characters don't

Example: Fire station burns down (situational irony!)

FORESHADOWING:
Hints about what will happen later
Example: Dark clouds = storm coming (literally or metaphorically)

ALLITERATION:
Repeated consonant sounds
Example: "Peter Piper picked a peck of pickled peppers"

SUPPLY AND DEMAND
-----------------

SIMPLE EXPLANATION:

DEMAND: How much people want to buy
- Price goes DOWN â†’ People buy MORE
- Price goes UP â†’ People buy LESS

SUPPLY: How much sellers want to sell
- Price goes UP â†’ Sellers make MORE
- Price goes DOWN â†’ Sellers make LESS

EQUILIBRIUM: Where supply meets demand
- The "perfect" price
- Amount people want to buy = amount sellers want to sell

REAL EXAMPLE - Concert Tickets:

Low Price ($20):
- Everyone wants tickets (high demand)
- Not enough tickets (low supply)
- Shortage! People can't get tickets

High Price ($500):
- Few people want tickets (low demand)
- Lots of tickets available (high supply)
- Surplus! Empty seats

Equilibrium ($100):
- Just right! All tickets sold
- No shortage, no surplus

WHY PRICES CHANGE:

Increase in Demand â†’ Price UP
Example: iPhone release â†’ everyone wants it â†’ price stays high

Increase in Supply â†’ Price DOWN
Example: Too many strawberries harvested â†’ price drops

Real World:
"This is why airplane tickets cost more during holidays (demand up) and less on random Tuesdays (demand down)!"

INFLATION
---------

WHAT IS IT:
General increase in prices over time. Your money buys less.

EXAMPLE:
- 1950: Movie ticket = $0.50
- 2024: Movie ticket = $15
That's inflation!

WHY IT HAPPENS:
1. Too much money in economy
2. Production costs increase
3. High demand for goods

EFFECTS:
Good:
- Small inflation (2-3%) = healthy economy
- Encourages spending (money loses value if you wait)

Bad:
- High inflation = can't afford things
- Savings lose value
- Wages don't keep up

SOLUTION:
Central banks (like Federal Reserve) control money supply and interest rates to keep inflation stable.

GRAMMAR BASICS
--------------

PARTS OF SPEECH:

1. NOUN: Person, place, thing
   - Common: book, city, teacher
   - Proper: John, Paris, Microsoft

2. VERB: Action or state
   - Action: run, jump, think
   - State: is, seem, appear

3. ADJECTIVE: Describes noun
   - big house, blue sky, happy person

4. ADVERB: Describes verb, adjective, or another adverb
   - Usually ends in -ly
   - runs quickly, very big, extremely happy

5. PRONOUN: Replaces noun
   - I, you, he, she, it, we, they
   - Possessive: my, your, his, her, its, our, their

6. PREPOSITION: Shows relationship
   - in, on, at, under, between, through

7. CONJUNCTION: Connects words
   - and, but, or, so, because

8. INTERJECTION: Exclamation
   - Wow! Oh! Ouch! Hey!

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:

Simple Sentence: One independent clause
"She reads books."

Compound Sentence: Two independent clauses
"She reads books, and he writes stories."

Complex Sentence: Independent + dependent clause
"Although it was raining, she went outside."

TENSES:

PRESENT:
- Simple: I eat
- Continuous: I am eating
- Perfect: I have eaten

PAST:
- Simple: I ate
- Continuous: I was eating
- Perfect: I had eaten

FUTURE:
- Simple: I will eat
- Continuous: I will be eating
- Perfect: I will have eaten

COMMON MISTAKES:

Their/There/They're:
- Their = possessive (their book)
- There = place (over there)
- They're = they are (they're happy)

Your/You're:
- Your = possessive (your car)
- You're = you are (you're smart)

Its/It's:
- Its = possessive (its tail)
- It's = it is (it's raining)
